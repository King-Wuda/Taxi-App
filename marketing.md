# TaxiRoute SA — Marketing Plan: First 100 Users

**Goal:** 100 real, active users (people who searched at least one route) within 60 days of launch.  
**Budget:** R0 cash. Time and hustle only.

---

## The Core Insight

Our users fall into two groups:
- **Unfamiliar passengers** — students, young professionals, tourists who want to use taxis but don't know how
- **Regular taxi users** — people who already know the routes but want to share them with others (parents, friends, coworkers)

The second group is our biggest lever. They already know the value of the information — they just haven't had a way to share it before.

---

## Phase 1: Weeks 1–2 — Seed with Your Own Network

Before any marketing, get 10 people you know personally to use it.

**Actions:**
- WhatsApp your own contacts — university friends, family, coworkers — with a message like:
  > "I built an app that tells you which taxi to take and what to pay. Takes 10 seconds. Can you test it for me? [link]"
- Ask each person to send it to one person they know who uses taxis
- Collect feedback: what did they search for that wasn't there?

**Target:** 10–15 users. Goal is feedback, not numbers.

---

## Phase 2: Weeks 2–4 — University Campuses

University students are the perfect early adopter: tech-comfortable, taxi-dependent, and active on social media.

**Actions:**

### WhatsApp groups (free, highest ROI)
- Join every university student WhatsApp group you can: res groups, faculty groups, SRC groups, part-time job groups
- Post once, naturally:
  > "For anyone who uses taxis to get to campus — there's finally an app that shows you which route to take and what the fare should be. Free to use. [link]"
- Do this for: Wits, UJ, TUT, UL, Unisa (Gauteng-focused first)

### Notice boards
- Print 20 A5 flyers (black and white, under R50 at any print shop — or free at a library)
- Put them on notice boards at: taxi ranks near campuses, campus entrances, res buildings
- Simple design: "Which taxi? How much? Find out in 10 seconds." + URL

### SRC / student media
- Email or DM the student newspaper at each campus (Wits Vuvuzela, UJ Ujamaa, etc.)
- Pitch: "Local student builds free app to make taxis accessible — would you cover it?"
- Student media is always looking for local stories and will often publish for free

---

## Phase 3: Weeks 3–5 — Community Facebook Groups

SA Facebook groups have millions of active users who are exactly your audience.

**Target groups (post in all of these):**
- "Soweto Community Forum"
- "Johannesburg Residents"
- "Pretoria Community Forum"
- "Life in Joburg"
- "Alexandra Township"
- "Tembisa Community"
- Any "expats in South Africa" groups (people who need taxi help most)

**Post format (adapt per group):**
> "For anyone tired of not knowing which taxi to take or getting overcharged — I built a free tool that tells you the route, the rank, and the correct fare. No app download needed, just a website. Currently covers Gauteng. Would love feedback from this community. [link]"

**Rules:**
- Read each group's rules before posting
- Don't post the same text copy-pasted — personalise slightly for each group
- Respond to every single comment, even just "thanks!"

**Target:** 30–40 users from Facebook alone.

---

## Phase 4: Weeks 4–6 — TikTok (Zero Budget, High Reach)

One good TikTok video can reach 50,000+ people organically. You don't need followers.

**Video ideas (film on your phone, vertical, under 60 seconds):**

1. **"Did you know you're being overcharged?"**  
   Show the Uber price vs the taxi price for the same route. End with: "This app tells you the correct taxi fare."

2. **"How to take a taxi in Joburg if you've never done it"**  
   Screen-record yourself searching a route on the app, then cut to the rank. Very practical, very shareable.

3. **"The taxi system explained in 60 seconds"**  
   Fast-paced explainer of how SA taxi ranks work. Educational content performs well.

4. **"I built an app to make SA taxis easier — here's why"**  
   Tell the story. Authentic founder content works on TikTok.

**Tags to use:** #SouthAfrica #Joburg #Soweto #Pretoria #TaxiLife #PublicTransport #SouthAfricanTikTok

**Posting schedule:** 1 video every 3–4 days for the first month.

---

## Phase 5: Weeks 5–8 — Reddit and Twitter/X

**Reddit:**
- Post in r/southafrica:
  > "I built a free tool to help people navigate the SA minibus taxi system — routes, ranks, and fares. Would love feedback."
- r/johannesburg, r/capetown — same approach
- Reddit rewards genuine, non-salesy posts. Be honest that it's your project and you want feedback.

**Twitter/X:**
- Post a thread: "I'm building an app to make SA taxis as easy as Uber. Here's why it matters [thread]"
- Tag: @BusinessInsiderSA, @TechCentralSA, @Memepool — SA tech accounts that sometimes RT local projects
- Search "taxi johannesburg" on Twitter and reply to people complaining about taxis with a useful, non-spammy mention of the app

---

## Phase 6: Ongoing — Referral Loop

Once you have 30+ users, activate word of mouth:

- Add a "Share this route" button to each route detail page
- WhatsApp share link: pre-filled message like "Found this taxi route on TaxiRoute SA: Bara → Joburg CBD, R14-18, 35-55 min. [link]"
- Ask 5 of your most engaged users to post about it once — offer to feature them as "community contributors" or early supporters

---

## What NOT to do

- **Don't pay for Facebook/Instagram ads yet** — too expensive per conversion for an MVP with no reviews or social proof
- **Don't try to go viral** — focus on 1 route, 1 campus, 1 group at a time
- **Don't post and ghost** — respond to every comment and message personally for the first 60 days. This is how you learn what people actually need.

---

## Tracking Progress

Keep a simple note with:
- Total users (check Supabase dashboard → Authentication)
- Top searches (which destinations people are looking for most — tells you what data to add next)
- Where each user came from (ask them directly in the first week, then use UTM links per channel)

Simple UTM links (free, just add to your URL):
- WhatsApp: `?utm_source=whatsapp`
- Facebook: `?utm_source=facebook`
- TikTok: `?utm_source=tiktok`

## After 100 Users

Once you have 100 users and real search data:
- You know which routes are being searched most → pitch those associations first
- You have a story to tell investors or grants (SA tech grants, Anzisha Prize, SEDA)
- You can approach student newspapers and tech blogs with "here's what we learned from our first 100 users"
